Chapter 6

Coal and Petroleum

Question 1

Discuss different varieties of coal and their characteristics.

Answer

The different varieties of coal are:

  1. Peat: Peat is a spongy type of organic matter and consists of fibre. Source of peat is dead plant material. It consists of 27% carbon.
  2. Lignite: Lignite is a young stage in coal formation which has 28-30% carbon. It is known as brown coal.
  3. Bituminous: It is a commonly used variety of coal. It is shiny black in colour and has no moisture content. It is household soft coal.
  4. Anthracite: It is the best form of coal which has less moisture content and is also known as smokeless coal. It is also known as hard coal.

Question 2

Why is conservation of fossil fuel so important? List the various method to conserve fossil fuel.

Answer

Conservation of fossil fuel is important because:

  1. Fossil fuels are the major source of energy.
  2. Burning them leads to major environmental problems such as air pollution, acid rain and global warming.
  3. They are exhaustible.

We can adopt the following methods to conserve fossil fuels:

  1. Walking down to nearby areas instead of going by a car or a scooter.
  2. Switching off the engine at traffic lights.
  3. Using car pool is a good way to reduce consumption of fossil fuels.

Question 3

List the uses of coal.

Answer

The various uses of coal are:

  1. Coal is used as fuel because of it is easy to burn and has a high calorific value.
  2. Coal is used to make coal gas, synthetic petrol, methane and coke.
  3. It is burned to heat water to produce steam that turn the turbine to produce electricity.
  4. It is used to manufacture other industrial compounds such as benzene, naphthalene, etc.
